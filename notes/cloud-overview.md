# Cloud Computing Overview

Cloud computing enables organizations to access computing resources such as
servers, storage, networking, and applications over the internet without
maintaining physical infrastructure on-site.

From a business and project management perspective, cloud computing supports
scalability, cost optimization, operational resilience, and faster delivery
of digital services.

## Why Organizations Adopt Cloud

- Reduced capital expenditure on hardware and data centers
- Improved scalability to meet changing demand
- Increased availability and reliability of systems
- Faster deployment of applications and services
- Support for remote access and distributed teams

## Cloud and Business Operations

Cloud platforms enable organizations to:
- Centralize data and systems
- Improve collaboration across teams
- Enhance disaster recovery and business continuity
- Support data-driven decision-making

## Relevance to Energy and Oil & Gas

In energy and oil & gas environments, cloud computing supports:
- Operational data management
- Training and simulation systems
- Enterprise resource planning (ERP)
- Business analytics and reporting
- Secure collaboration across locations

## Summary

Cloud computing is a foundational component of modern digital infrastructure,
enabling organizations to align technology capabilities with business strategy,
operational efficiency, and long-term growth.
